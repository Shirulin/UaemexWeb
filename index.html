ADSL (Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line)

Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line (ADSL) is a type of digital subscriber line (DSL) technology, a data communications technology that enables faster data transmission over copper telephone lines than a conventional voiceband modem can provide.

Operating Principles

The key characteristic of ADSL is that it utilizes the existing twisted-pair copper telephone lines to deliver high-speed internet access without interrupting regular telephone service. This is achieved through frequency division multiplexing (FDM).

  • Frequency Separation: A standard telephone line has a much wider frequency range capacity than what is used for voice calls. Voice communication uses the 0-4 kHz frequency band. ADSL technology takes advantage of the unused higher frequencies to transmit data.
  • Data and Voice Channels: The available frequency spectrum is divided into:
    1. POTS (Plain Old Telephone Service): 0–4 kHz reserved for voice calls.
    2. Upstream Data: Medium frequencies for uploading data.
    3. Downstream Data: Higher frequencies for downloading data.
  • Asymmetric Nature: Download speeds are much higher than upload speeds, fitting typical user behavior.

FTTH (Fiber to the Home)

Fiber to the Home (FTTH) is the delivery of a communications signal over optical fiber from the operator's switching equipment all the way to a home or business, replacing existing copper infrastructure such as telephone wires and coaxial cable.

Operating Principles

FTTH is fundamentally different from ADSL because it uses fiber-optic cables instead of copper wires. Data is transmitted as pulses of light, which allows for incredibly high speeds and signal quality over long distances.

  • Data Transmission via Light: Information is encoded into light pulses from lasers or LEDs.
  • Total Internal Reflection: Signals travel inside the fiber with minimal loss over distance.
  • Active vs. Passive Optical Networks:
    • AON: Dedicated fiber per customer, electrically powered switches.
    • PON: Shared fiber split to multiple users, more cost-effective.
  • Symmetric Speeds: Upload and download speeds are equal, perfect for video calls, cloud storage, and gaming.
